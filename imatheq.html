Homogeneous Function

  • Verify Eulers Theorem, when

    • f(x,y,z)=x2+y2+z2

    Example 1

    Solution:

    Given Data:

    f(x,y,z)=x2+y2+z2

    f(kx,ky,kz)=k2x2+k2y2+k2z2

    =k2(x2+y2+z2)

    ∴f(kx,ky,kz)=k2f(x,y,z)

    f(x, y, z) is a homogeneous of degree 2.

    ∴      n=2

    To prove x∂f∂x+y∂f∂y+z∂f∂z=nf=2f

    ∂f∂x=∂∂x(x2+y2+z2)

    ∵    d(xn)=nxn-1

    =2x+0+0

    ∂f∂x=2x

    ∴      x∂f∂x=2x2                       ......(1)

    ∂f∂y=∂∂y(x2+y2+z2)

    =0+2y+0

    ∂f∂y=2y

    ∴      y∂f∂y=2y2                       ......(2)

    ∂f∂z=∂∂z(x2+y2+z2)

    =0+2z+0

    ∂f∂z=2z

    ∴      z∂f∂z=2z2                       ......(3)

    (1)+(2)+(3)⇒

    x∂f∂x+y∂f∂y+z∂f∂z=2x2 +2y2 +2z2 

    =2 (x2+y2+z2)

    x∂f∂x+y∂f∂y+z∂f∂z=2f

    Hence Proved.
