Homogeneous Function

A function f(x, y) is said to be a homogeneous function of degree 'n' in x and y, if

Example 1.

Determine the radius of curvature of the straight line.

Example 1

$$ \eqalign {f(k{\bf{x}},ky) &= a{(k{\bf{x}})^2} + 2h(k{\bf{x}})(ky) + b{(ky)^2} \\ &= a{k^2}{{\bf{x}}^2} + 2h{k^2}{\bf{x}}y + b{k^2}{y^2} \\ &= {k^2}(a{{\bf{x}}^2} + 2h{\bf{x}}y + b{y^2}) \\ f(k{\bf{x}},ky) &= {k^2}f({\bf{x}},y) }$$

f(x, y) is s homogeneous function of degree 2

Example 2

$$ \eqalign {f({\bf{x}},y) &= {\sin ^{ - 1}}\left( {{{\bf{x}} \over y}} \right) + {\cos ^{ - 1}}\left( {{{\bf{x}} \over y}} \right) \\ f(k{\bf{x}},ky) &= {\sin ^{ - 1}}\left( {{{k{\bf{x}}} \over {ky}}} \right) + {\cos ^{ - 1}}\left( {{{k{\bf{x}}} \over {ky}}} \right) \\ &= {\sin ^{ - 1}}\left( {{{\bf{x}} \over y}} \right) + {\cos ^{ - 1}}\left( {{{\bf{x}}\over y}} \right) \\ f(k{\bf{x}},ky) &= {k^0}f({\bf{x}},y) }$$
